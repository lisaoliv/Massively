Case Analysis  ITT Skills LLC

Current Situation 

ITT Skills LLC is an international IT recruiting, training, and consulting provider headquartered in Nashua, NH, admits students from Massachusetts. The organization relies on digital processes, email and shared drives to collect and store personal information (PI), which makes strong data governance and security controls essential.

The key decision makers are the Founder of ITT Skills LLC, the Compliance Officer / Training Coordinator, and the Lead Training Coordinator, who jointly control the organizations datahandling practices and student access to live Zoom classes. The immediate task is to select a lawful method to collect and store Massachusetts Social Security numbers and copies of governmentissued identification without relying on shared folders, static passwords, or unauthenticated links. The issue has surfaced because the current storage process exposed personal information through unencrypted transmission and open OneDrive links, leading to student complaints, questions about contract compliance, and statutory risk under 201 CMR 17.00. A decision is required before the next Monday morning class session, as the Lead Training Coordinator has withheld the Zoom access link from a student whose tuition is already funded, pending resolution of how regulated data will be handled.

 

Step 2. In Depth Analysis 

The business problem is selecting a compliant method to collect and store Social Security numbers and license images for Massachusetts residents. ITT Skills LLC currently relies on unencrypted transmission paths, unauthenticated OneDrive links, and a generic static password created for one student, leaving other Massachusetts residents exposed to the same weaknesses.

This problem is important to the organization because exposure of regulated identifiers creates breach liability under 201 CMR 17.00, damages student trust, and disrupts service delivery. Students cannot safely transmit regulated identifiers into a system that fails statutory dataprotection requirements while their funded access to training depends on doing so.

The current IT environment contributes to the problem through unauthenticated file sharing, lack of encryption controls, and dependence on a single shared password pattern. No rolebased access, logging, or secure upload workflow is in place to govern how regulated identifiers are handled.

Organizational practices reinforce these technical weaknesses. The storage workflow has no clearly assigned owner, formal risk assessment, or structured training, and a culture of adhoc file sharing discourages adoption of formal controls.

The situation developed over time as the organization adopted consumer cloud tools without technical governance or documented datahandling standards. A generic datasecurity policy was distributed to students, but it did not specify technical safeguards, access controls, or secure transmission practices, and the password convention used for students personal information was neither contextappropriate nor aligned with established security guidance.

Ethical considerations include duties to prevent foreseeable harm, maintain the confidentiality of regulated identifiers, communicate risks accurately, and implement safeguards proportionate to the sensitivity of the data collected. Continuing to handle regulated identifiers without appropriate controls raises ethical concerns about consent, transparency, and professional diligence.

Decision 

Proposed Solution to the Business Problem & Ethical Aspects to Consider

The best option is building a Salesforce intake workflow with identity-bound authentication, MFA, encryption, and audit logging. This approach meets all evaluation criteria: it aligns with 201 CMR 17.00, mitigates security gaps created by shared folders, maintains operational continuity, and is supported by strong third-party assurance (SOC/ISO/BCR certifications).

This solution replaces uncontrolled shared-drive storage with a secure, identity-bound system that encrypts intake, enforces authenticated access, and logs all handling activity. It eliminates the architectural vulnerabilities that caused concerns about data exposure and onboarding delays, ensuring regulated identifiers are handled in a compliant and transparent way. 

Ethical considerations include protecting individuals sensitive identifiers, minimizing the risk of harm caused by data exposure, ensuring fair and consistent intake procedures, and avoiding unnecessary barriers that impact learners. The decision prioritizes privacy, safety, and responsible stewardship of regulated data.
