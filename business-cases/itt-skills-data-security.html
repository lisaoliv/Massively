Case Study Scenario:
ITT Skills LLC Student Data Security

You are a Massachusetts student whose access to the class link is conditioned on uploading personal information (name, Social Security number, and photo ID) into a system that lacks several statutory minimum security safeguards.

Situation

  1. ITT Skills LLC is an international IT training provider, headquartered in New Hampshire, that admits students, like you, from Massachusetts.
  2. ITT Skills LLC stores student registration paperwork digitally on a shared drive that has been publicly accessible.
  3. Registration documents include students’ name, address, date of birth, Social Security number, and photocopies of government‑issued licenses/IDs.
  4. After you raised data‑security concerns about this link, the company emailed you a static password and a password‑protected file, while continuing to rely on the same basic approach.
  5. The Zoom link to your next class is withheld until you upload your SSN and photo ID.

Deliverables

You are tasked with informing the training provider:

  1. why their system still does not meet the statutory minimum under 201 CMR 17.00, and
  2. recommending a solution that will guide the organization toward a comprehensive Written Information Security Program (WISP).
