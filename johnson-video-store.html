Johnson Video Store
Relational Database & SQL Analytics

PostgreSQL data model and queries for a video rental business.

Project overview

This project replaces handwritten rental slips with a structured PostgreSQL database for Johnson Video Store. It models movies, distributors, inventory copies, customers and rentals, seeds sample data, and uses joins plus aggregates to track late fees, revenue and customer trends.

Deliverables

  • ERD covering movies, distributors, inventory, rentals and customers
  • Metadata schema detailing datatypes, constraints, and key relationships
  • DDL scripts to build tables, indexes and primary/foreign keys
  • DML inserts populating at least 20 sample rows per table
  • SQL queries answering business questions about late returns, revenue and popular titles

Role and tools

  • Role: Data modeler and SQL developer
  • Tools: PostgreSQL, pgAdmin 4, ERD modeling

Business questions

  • Who returns movies late most often and generates late fees
  • What revenue rental and late fees generate over a year
  • Which genres and titles rent most frequently
  • Which distributors supply high performing titles
  • Which customers rent more than 10 movies per year

Data model

I created an ERD with core entities such as movie, customer, rental, dvd, video, distributor and customer_account. Bridge tables connect movies with actors, directors and awards. Surrogate primary keys and foreign keys support one to many and many to many relationships for inventory and rentals.

Implementation

  • Created DDL scripts to build all tables and constraints in PostgreSQL
  • Seeded at least 20 rows per table with realistic movies, customers and rentals
  • Added indexes and uniqueness constraints for inventory numbers and customer accounts

Analytics queries

Example query: customers with high late returns.


SELECT c.customer_id,
       c.customer_name,
       COUNT(*) AS late_rentals
FROM rental r
JOIN customer c
  ON r.customer_id = c.customer_id
WHERE r.return_date > r.rental_date + INTERVAL '3 days'
GROUP BY c.customer_id, c.customer_name
ORDER BY late_rentals DESC;

Example query: total revenue from rentals and late fees in the last year.


SELECT DATE_TRUNC('year', r.rental_date) AS rental_year,
       SUM(rental_fee + late_fee)       AS total_revenue
FROM rental r
WHERE r.rental_date >= CURRENT_DATE - INTERVAL '1 year'
GROUP BY rental_year
ORDER BY rental_year;

Outcomes

The database supports consistent reporting on rental activity, revenue and customer behavior. The project demonstrates end to end relational design, SQL implementation and business-focused analytics.
